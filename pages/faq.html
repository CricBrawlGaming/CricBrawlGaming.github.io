FAQ

What is CricBrawl?

CricBrawl is a fantasy cricket platform where users can create their own virtual cricket teams by selecting real players from upcoming matches. Points are earned based on the actual performance of these players in live matches. It's a skill-based game where strategic thinking and cricket knowledge are key.

How do I participate in a fantasy cricket contest on CricBrawl?
  • Sign Up/Login: Create an account or log in if you already have one.
  • Select a Match: Choose an upcoming cricket match from the available options.
  • Create Your Team: Use your cricket expertise to select a team of 11 players within a specified budget.
  • Join Contests: Enter your team into various contests for a chance to win prizes.
  • Track Performance: Monitor your team's performance live as the match progresses.
How do I deposit and withdraw funds?

To deposit funds, navigate to the "Wallet" section, select the deposit option, and choose your preferred payment method. To withdraw winnings, go to the "Wallet" section, choose the withdraw option, and follow the prompts to transfer funds to your bank account or preferred payment method. Verification may be required for security purposes.

How are points calculated in CricBrawl?

Points are awarded based on the real-life performance of the selected players in the match. Key actions such as runs scored, wickets taken, catches, and other significant contributions earn points according to CricBrawl's scoring system. To learn more about it, refer to Fantasy Points calculation page.

Can I change my team after creating it?

Yes, you can make changes to your team until the match deadline. This includes substituting players, changing the captain or vice-captain, and adjusting the team composition based on the latest information and strategies.

What is the minimum required entrants for a contest to run?

Every public contest on CricBrawl has a minimum required number of entrants. Once this threshold is met, the contest is guaranteed to run, and the prize pool will dynamically adjust based on the total number of participants.

Is CricBrawl legal and safe?

Yes, CricBrawl is a legal platform where skill-based fantasy cricket is played. It is compliant with relevant laws and ensures a secure and fair gaming environment. User verification and secure payment methods are in place to protect users.

What is responsible gaming on CricBrawl?

CricBrawl promotes responsible gaming by encouraging users to play within their means and providing tools to manage their gaming habits. Users can set deposit limits and self-exclude if necessary. Support for responsible gaming practices is available on the platform.

How can I ensure fair play on CricBrawl?

CricBrawl is committed to providing a fair playing environment. Users must be 18+, have only one account, and verify their identity for secure transactions. The use of bots or automated tools is strictly prohibited, and collusion between users is not allowed. Any violations can be reported to the support team for investigation.

What are the different player experience levels?
CricBrawl assigns experience badges to players based on their participation:
  • Beginner Level: Less than 50 contests entered.
  • Intermediate Level: 50 or more contests entered but not meeting the criteria for Highly-Experienced.
  • Highly-Experienced Level: More than 1,000 contests entered or multiple high-value prizes won.
Is There Tax Implication If I Win?

Any winnings of $600 or more in a calendar year are subject to federal tax reporting. CricBrawl will issue a Form 1099 to you and the IRS if your total winnings exceed this amount. You are responsible for reporting your winnings on your federal income tax return, regardless of whether you receive a Form 1099.

What States Are Allowed?

CricBrawl is committed to adhering to all state regulations regarding fantasy sports. As such, the availability of our services varies depending on state laws. Currently, CricBrawl is available in the following states: California, Florida, Georgia, Illinois, Kentucky, Minnesota, Nebraska, New Mexico, North Carolina, North Dakota, Oklahoma, Oregon, Rhode Island, South Carolina, South Dakota, Texas, Utah, Wisconsin.
